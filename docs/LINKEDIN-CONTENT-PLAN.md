# FDE-Toolkit LinkedIn Content Plan

## Objective

Make FDE-Toolkit synonymous with a measurable operating problem: enterprise teams can generate code quickly, but the path from a client ask to an approved, merged pull request still takes weeks.

## Content pillars

1. Delivery velocity with governance: the 48-hour PR, approval evidence, and sandbox-to-PR conversion.
2. Customer proximity without product entropy: forward-deployed engineering, design partners, and preventing permanent customer forks.
3. The artifact flywheel: knowledge reuse, reference solutions, decision records, and compounding delivery IP.
4. SI operating economics: active FDEs per seat, practice expansion, delivery margin, and expansion-driven NRR.

1. State the tension.
2. Reframe the common assumption.
3. Offer a metric, model, or artifact.
4. Ask readers to share their baseline.
5. Link to the relevant FDE-Toolkit article.

## Launch post draft

AI can generate code in minutes.

Yet a concrete enterprise client request can still take two to four weeks to become an approved, merged pull request.

The delay is rarely typing. It is the chain of trust around the change: what the client asked for, which constraints were agreed, what users validated, who approved promotion, and which part is reusable.

A better North Star for forward-deployed engineering is median time from client ask to approved, merged PR.

The target we are designing around at FDE-Toolkit is under 48 hours, without bypassing engineering or governance controls.

What is the current median cycle time in your organization?
